Consider a two body break-up problem (decay/explosion). <br>
In classical mechanics, E = p^2/2m, with conservation of momentum we get that the lighter mass shares the bigger part of decay/explosion energy Q. <br>
In extreme case where m1>>m2, m1 gets all the energy while m2 gets zero energy.

However, with relativety, this is not the case in general. Actually often the exact opposite happens, the heavy mass gets almost all the energy.<br>
This program calculates the energy and velocity after decay, with input: m1, m2, Q. And it also visualizes how much relativity is affecting the results.
